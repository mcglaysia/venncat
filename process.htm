The Process

To accomplish the task, what steps should the learners go through? Use the numbered list format in your web editor to automatically number the steps in the procedure. Describing this section well will help other teachers to see how your lesson flows and how they might adapt it for their own use, so the more detail and care you put into this, the better. Remember that this whole document is addressed to the student, however, so describe the steps using the second person.

  1. First you'll be assigned to a team of 3 students...
  2. Once you've picked a role to play....
  3. ... and so on.

Learners will access the on-line resources that you've identifed as they go through the Process. You may have a set of links that everyone looks at as a way of developing background information, or not. If you break learners into groups, embed the links that each group will look at within the description of that stage of the process. (Note, this is a change from the older WebQuest templates which included a separate Resources section. It's now clear that the resources belong in the Process section rather than alone.)

In the Process block, you might also provide some guidance on how to organize the information gathered. This advice could suggestions to use flowcharts, summary tables, concept maps, or other organizing structures. The advice could also take the form of a checklist of questions to analyze the information with, or things to notice or think about. If you have identified or prepared guide documents on the Web that cover specific skills needed for this lesson (e.g. how to brainstorm, how to prepare to interview an expert), link them to this section.

Journals:

 

  1. Think about all of the myths that people believed about exploring into the “Unknown”……sea monsters, falling off the end of the world, ships exploding into flames, seas churning and boiling…

Write about how you felt preparing to go on exploration into the “Unknown”.  How did it feel to leave your family?  Did you think you would ever return?  What kinds of experiences did you think you would have?  Why were you going?

 

  1. When you wake up one morning, three of the crew members are gone! 

Write about what other people are saying happened to the three men, and what you think happened.

  1. While you are working on the sails, you get to know another young sailor, who tells you a great story about his (or her) uncle who was also an explorer.

Write the story in your journal, so you won’t forget it.

 

 

  1. On your return write your final journal about your thoughts and feelings.  Include your thoughts about the following (was the expedition successful why/why not, would you take another journey, how you feel about being back in your home country)

 

 

Power Point Presentation:

 

Here are the components that must be in the Power Point presentation:

 

  • Mission Statement

  • Brief History of Explorer/Expedition

  • Vehicle diagram – You may use paint program or sketch and scan

  • Create a map showing the route

  • Navigational Equipment

  • Provisions

  • Research of the natives encountered

  • 3 arguments to support the success of your mission

  • Bibliography – Use formats found on Writers Express page 231

 

Mission Statement:

 

Write a Mission Statement (2-3 sentences) that states the main objective of your expedition.  Your statement should answer the questions listed below.

1)      Why did your group decide to go on this expedition?

2)      Who funded your voyage?

3)      What did you hope to accomplish?

 

History of Explorer/Expedition:

 

Write a short report (2-3 paragraphs) about the explorer or expedition you are researching.  Your report should answer the questions below:

 

1)      What is the explorer’s name?

2)      Where was he born/

3)      Why did he want to explore?

4)      What was the purpose of the expedition?

5)      What country was supporting the voyage?

6)      What was the desired destination of the exploration?

7)      What were the findings of this expedition?

8)      What is this explorer famous for accomplishing?

 

Vehicle Diagram:

 

 

You may use the paint program or sketch and scan a diagram of your vehicle/transportation.  Make sure you label the important parts of your diagram.

 

Map of Voyage:

 

Use the Map Makers Toolkit to show the route of your expedition.  Be sure to have a title.

 

Navigational Equipment:

 

Research at least 3 navigational devices.  Write a paragraph describing how each device works and what it is used for.  Include a drawing or download a picture for each device.

 

Provisions:

 

Research the supplies that were taken on this expedition.  Make a list of the supplies needed.  Include any other notes on how food was preserved and/or rationed (if you find this).

 

Natives Encountered:

 

Write 2/3 paragraphs on the natives encountered.  Choose 2 of the following topics to focus on:

  • home/habitat

  • food

  • art/ceremonies

  • clothing

 

ARGUMENTS: All group members to work on arguments together!!

 

Write 3 arguments in support of why your expedition was successful.  These arguments will be used to convince the King and Queen to help fund further exploration. 

 

Bibliography:

 

Use the formats found in WRITERS EXPRESS page 231

 

  

Presentation Format:

 

Your group will use Power Point Presentation to present your findings to the King and Queen on your return.  Each crewmember will be responsible for participating in the oral presentation.  Your group will decide how to present the information in an organized manner.
